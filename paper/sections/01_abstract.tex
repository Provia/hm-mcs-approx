\begin{abstract}
Type error localization in Hindley--Milner systems can be formulated as a
minimum-weight Minimum Correction Subset problem over unsatisfiable
type-equality constraints. This project studies whether a lazy MUS-guided
greedy hitting-set algorithm gives useful approximation behaviour on
synthetic HM-style constraint systems. We implement the greedy algorithm, an
exact Z3 Optimize baseline, and a brute-force sanity checker for tiny
instances. The evaluation covers 300 generated instances across three
structural families: single planted conflicts, overlapping conflicts on a
shared type variable, and constructor-level function--list mismatches. Across
all instances, greedy matches the exact optimum with approximation ratio
$1.0$. This does not show general greedy optimality. Instead, it diagnoses the
current generators as structurally simple: their MUS hypergraphs do not
exercise the standard worst-case behaviour of greedy hitting set. Runtime
results further show that lazy greedy overhead is driven more by MUS discovery
count than by total constraint count.
\end{abstract}
